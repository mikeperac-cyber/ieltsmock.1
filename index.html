Reading Test

Passage 1: The Honeybee

"The queen is the only fertile female and her sole purpose is to lay eggs, up to 2,000 a day during the spring."

3. A queen bee lays exactly 2,000 eggs every single day of the year. [ TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN ]
⚠️ Trap Alert! The answer is FALSE. The text says "up to" (meaning a maximum, not exactly) and "during the spring" (not every single day of the year). IELTS loves to change "some" to "all" or "up to" to "exactly" to trick you!

👩‍🏫 Teacher's Guide for Gulsum (A1/A2 to Band 5->7)

  • Grammar: To get a Band 7, you must use "complex sentences". This means using words like Although, Because, While, Which.
    Example: "Although technology is useful, it can be stressful."
  • Vocabulary: Don't memorize long, difficult words. Learn "collocations" (words that go together). For example, we say "heavy rain" (not "thick rain") and "make a decision" (not "do a decision").
  • Exam Strategy: In Reading, if a question uses the word "ALL" or "EVERY", it is usually FALSE. The text usually says "SOME" or "MANY". Always check the details!
